0.1 Think like a programmer

Procedural Thinking

Taking a big problem and breaking the solution in several steps.
Procedural thinking involves creating perfectly clear and unambiguous instructions for a computer to follow.
It may be easy to tell a person how to sort a deck of cards, but getting a computer to do that requires more thought. But once I've learned to think this way I'll find that computers can do it much faster.

Abstract Thinking

Finding similarities, or generalities amongst differents concepts.
If 1+1=2, therefore one apple plus one apple gives two apples. I can also use abstraction to avo√Ød repetition with classes ( in stylesheets) or use an element for another porpose than the forstly inteded. (cf Kim's memories homework

Debugging

Mistakes is anavoidable part of programming, I have to systematicaly fix them. Ussually the process looks like this:

  • List the evidences : What (makes this programm) fails ?
  • Make theories about causes
  • Test the theories
  • Fix the problem :p

Technological Empathy

How the computeur see thinks, how it do works.
To programm anything substantial, it's important to understant what a computer is, what it's good and bad at doing. A computeur is a tool, a program and a programming language also, I must know them.

Systems Thinking

Take one big idea and break it down in smaller pieces.
Create a plan (on paper) for how my program will work. It leads to see the big-picture, how the different pieces can/could work together and make decisions.
